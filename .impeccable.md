## Design Context

### Users
Tech recruiters and hiring managers at product-driven companies. They are evaluating candidates who can bridge design and engineering — people who ship high-fidelity interaction design, not just mockups. They browse portfolios quickly, often on laptops during work hours, and are looking for evidence of craft, taste, and technical depth. The job to be done: convince them in under 30 seconds that Kathleen obsesses over both the "why" behind user flows and the "how" behind implementation.

### Brand Personality
**Cinematic, Inquisitive, Architectural.**

- **Cinematic**: The portfolio is a curated gallery, not a list of links. Film grain, atmospheric depth, and controlled lighting create a sense of place. Every transition feels like a scene cut.
- **Inquisitive**: An eager, curious mind that digs into problems. The design should feel like it's always exploring — subtle reveals, progressive disclosure, details that reward attention.
- **Architectural**: Structural precision inherited from a CSIE background. Layouts feel load-bearing, not decorative. Spacing is deliberate. Nothing is arbitrary.

### Emotional Target
"She possesses a sophisticated design eye and a deep technical curiosity. She doesn't just build interfaces — she obsesses over the 'why' behind the user flow and the 'how' behind the implementation."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode, committed. Deep near-black canvas (#131313) with muted sage green accent (#b4c29f). The darkness lets project screenshots become the heroes of the page.
- **Texture**: Film grain overlay is a core stylistic choice — it adds soul and physicality to the digital experience. Lean into it.
- **References**: Linear (fluid, physics-based interactions that feel functional, not decorative), Apple product pages (controlled reveals, premium pacing, scroll-driven storytelling).
- **Anti-references**: Generic high-saturation SaaS templates. Colorful bento-box layouts. Flat, depthless pages that feel like themed Bootstrap. Anything that screams "template."
- **Motion philosophy**: Animations must provide functional feedback and premium feel. Physics-based, not spring-bounce. Every animation should prove capability — this is a portfolio for a UI/UX internship, and the interactions are the resume.

### Design Principles

1. **Atmosphere over decoration.** Depth comes from lighting, texture, and spatial relationships — not from adding more elements. If it doesn't contribute to the cinematic feel, remove it.

2. **Reward attention.** The portfolio should have layers of detail that reveal themselves on closer inspection. Hover states that shift, scroll positions that unlock, typography that breathes. Recruiters scanning quickly get the impression; those who linger get the proof.

3. **Structure is visible.** Layouts should feel engineered — precise grids, deliberate asymmetry, clear information hierarchy. The CSIE background should be felt in the bones of the design, not just listed in the About section.

4. **Motion earns its place.** Every animation must be functional (guiding attention, providing feedback, communicating state) or atmospheric (reinforcing the cinematic tone). No animation for animation's sake. Physics-based easing over bounce/elastic.

5. **The work is the hero.** Project screenshots, case studies, and demonstrated skills are the focal point. The portfolio chrome exists to frame them, not compete with them. Sage green accents draw the eye to what matters.
